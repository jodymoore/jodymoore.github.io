Running Start Club

The RVC Running Start Program allows for students to advance into their careers following high school graduation! Students can obtain an Associate degree meanwhile still earning their high school diploma, and then they can transfer to a four-year institution with the equipped social skills to be successful. When a student is involved in the Running Start program, they get free tuition! No debt after college, and you graduate sooner! This is every student's dream opportunity. Math Lab

The RVC Math Lab is an astounding resource for many math students. The math lab welcomes drop-ins and is located on the bottom floor of the Karl J. Jacobs Center for Science and Math. For you convenience, there is a variety of educated math tutors, computers, and printers. We offer graphing calculator rentals each semester, and hold textbooks on each math course, for use in the lab. Need help learning how to use your new calculator? Come in, and we will be happy to show you all the steps! We take your math education very serious here at RVC. If you need additional help in your mathematical pursuits then you have come to the right place! Whether you are taking a math class on campus or online, the math lab and their tutors are available to any students who are registered into math courses. Check the math lab/tutoring schedule, and come meet our wonderful tutors!

TRIO Services

Rock Valley College is proud to have our Trio Student Support Services. They service over 140 low-income students, and students with disabilities. Trio provides instructional support and help students overcome barriers associated with class, academic, social, and cultural challenges; in order to complete and advance in their educational pursuits. We assign life coaches, mentorship, support, goal setting, study skills, and much more. Don’t let your financial situation stop you from achieving your goals! We have a laptop lending program, offer financial and economic literacy workshops, and assist with Financial Aid. Once you are a Trio student, you will be able to enjoy the perks of taking bus tours to different colleges around the state of Illinois, as well as enjoy our social events here on campus and off campus! Last year, students and staff enjoyed a night of bowling and pizza, had Stress Free Day at RVC during finals week, and traveled to different Universities throughout Chicago. We have our own math, English, and tech tutors who provide additional free assistance to the members of the Trio Student Support Services. We welcome new registered students, and encourage you to reach out to our department by filling out our online application.

Physical Education Center (PEC)

Rock Valley College encourages students, faculty, and staff to participate and utilize our newly remodeled facilities. In 2009, RVC spent $13 million to remodel and update thePEC building. The Fitness Lab is equipped with cardiovascular machines, weight lifting machines, and free weight equipment. The gymnasium has new athletic wood gym floors, a 1/10 mile three lane track that converts from a volleyball court to a basketball court. The Dance/Exercise Studio was expanded and moved onto the main floor. With more accessibility in mind, restrooms are now available on the main floor, and the building’s entrances are now handicap accessible. Above all, the most important aspect in the new design, is the “Green Build” focus behind the renovation. The school reached its goal of achieving a Gold Level L.E.E.D. ( Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design) certification. The construction is the first in Rockford to be built with recycled products in order to reduce energy consumptions. The building also provides fitness, wellness, and sport development courses that can help students achieve many different certificates such as: personal training, coaching, exercise science, and sports management.

Starlight Theatre

Rock Valley College had its very first production in 1967, called Finian’s Rainbow, which turned out to be very successful. Many people from the community gathered around in lawn chairs and admired the production. At the time, RVC had a beautiful farm landscape with serene ponds where students would perform musicals. By 2003 the impressive 135, 000 square foot Starlight Theater was built. The theater was acknowledged by architectural critic Blair Kamin as having “a marvel of engineering and technology.” The architectural design features a 200-foot retractable roof that opened like a blooming flower. Audience members can appreciate views of the night sky without weather interrupting over the last 140 productions held at the Starlight Theater. It takes many volunteers, cast, and crews to run a whole production. The theater department offers a wide variety of over 35,000 costume rentals, made here at our very own costume shop! The theatrical costumes and historical garments are available for rental by other institutions and individuals. The Starlight theater allows students and volunteers to work together as a community by displaying their talents. If you would like to participate in the next production visit the “Starlight Theater page”.
